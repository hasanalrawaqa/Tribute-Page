Suleiman the Magnificent

The Tenth Sultan Of The Ottoman Empire

Here's a time line of Sultan Suleiman the Magnificent:

  • 1494 - Born in Trabzon, Turkey to Selim I and his wife Hafsa Sultan.
  • 1512 - Serves as governor of Manisa province and gains valuable experience in administration and military strategy.
  • 1520 - Becomes Sultan of the Ottoman Empire at the age of 26 after the death of his father, Selim I
  • 1521 - Conquers Belgrade, a major fortress of the Christian world, opening the way for Ottoman expansion into Europe.
  • 1522 - Captures Rhodes from the Knights Hospitaller after a six-month siege, securing Ottoman control over the eastern Mediterranean.
  • 1525 - Defeats the Polish-Lithuanian army at the Battle of Orsza, marking the beginning of Ottoman expansion into eastern Europe.
  • 1526 - Defeats the Hungarian army at the Battle of Mohács, solidifying Ottoman rule over Hungary.
  • 1529 - Leads an unsuccessful siege of Vienna, Austria, which marks the furthest extent of Ottoman expansion into Europe.
  • 1532 - Defeats the Habsburgs in the Battle of Konya, securing Ottoman control over central Europe.
  • 1534 - Captures Tunis, extending Ottoman rule into the Mediterranean and establishing a base for further expansion in North Africa.
  • 1535 - Executes his son Mustafa in order to prevent a potential challenge to his rule.
  • 1541 - Conquers Buda, the capital of Hungary, and establishes Ottoman control over the region.
  • 1543 - Sends a fleet to capture the Portuguese-controlled city of Diu in India, marking the Ottoman Empire's first foray into the Indian Ocean.
  • 1550 - Orders the construction of the Galata Bridge in Istanbul, which becomes a vital artery for commerce and transportation in the city.
  • 1553 - Begins construction on the Süleymaniye Mosque in Istanbul, which becomes one of the most significant architectural works of the Ottoman Empire.
  • 1566 - Dies at the age of 72 while on campaign in Hungary. His son Selim II succeeds him as Sultan.
